# ADR-003: Immutable Cash Sessions with Owner Adjustments

## Status

Accepted

## Date

2026-06-24

## Context

The Phase 1 MVP centers on daily cash closing. The product must preserve trust in historical financial data while allowing the owner to correct mistakes found after a day is closed. The PRD requires closed cash sessions to remain historical and says later adjustments must be visible to the owner.

## Decision

Closed cash sessions will be immutable. During an open session, authorized users may create and edit operational entries such as payment totals, expenses, withdrawals, and attendance. When the session is final-closed — by the owner or, per [ADR-005](adr-005.md), the cashier — the API stores a closing snapshot with calculated sales, withdrawals, expenses, personnel cost, and estimated profit. After close, corrections will be represented as owner-only adjustment records linked to the closed session and included in owner-visible history and reports. Who may perform the close action is a separate concern from who may adjust a closed session: the latter stays owner-only regardless of who closed it.

## Alternatives Considered

### Alternative 1: Reopen closed sessions

- **Description**: Allow the owner to reopen a closed session, edit underlying entries, and close it again.
- **Pros**: Familiar edit workflow and fewer adjustment concepts in the UI.
- **Cons**: Makes historical numbers harder to audit and requires versioning to explain what changed.
- **Why rejected**: The pilot depends on owner trust in closed historical data, and visible adjustments provide a clearer audit path.

### Alternative 2: No post-close changes

- **Description**: Prevent any changes after final close.
- **Pros**: Simplest state machine and strongest immutability.
- **Cons**: Operational mistakes are likely in a manual pilot and would require workarounds outside the system.
- **Why rejected**: The system must replace paper without forcing the owner to abandon it when a correction is needed.

### Alternative 3: Cashier reopen or adjust

- **Description**: Allow both owner and cashier users to reopen or adjust closed sessions.
- **Pros**: Reduces owner workload.
- **Cons**: Weakens control over sensitive financial history.
- **Why rejected**: Cashiers should prepare operational data, while the owner controls final financial records.

## Consequences

### Positive

- Preserves an auditable record of each final close.
- Supports realistic correction workflows without mutating historical snapshots.
- Reinforces the owner-only boundary for sensitive financial changes.

### Negative

- Reports must account for both closing snapshots and adjustments.
- The UI needs to explain adjustments clearly to avoid confusing users.

### Risks

- Adjustment math can diverge from the original close if not modeled explicitly; mitigate by storing adjustment type, amount, reason, creator, and timestamp.
- Users may expect direct editing; mitigate with clear copy and an owner-focused adjustment screen.

## Implementation Notes

Model cash sessions with states such as `OPEN` and `CLOSED`. Store close-time totals on the session or a related closing snapshot. Use a `CashSessionAdjustment` table for post-close corrections with owner-only create permission. Monthly reports should calculate from closed snapshots plus approved adjustments.
