# ADR-005: Cashier-Enabled Cash Session Close

## Status

Accepted

## Date

2026-07-04

## Context

ADR-004 restricted final cash session close to owners only, reasoning that non-owner control over sensitive financial records was too risky for the pilot. In practice, the owner is not always present at closing time, and requiring owner confirmation for every close would block the daily closing workflow the pilot depends on (see PRD success metric: closing completed in under 10 minutes on at least 80% of days). The reference frontend (`apps/web-admin/docs/design/index.html`) also exposes a single "Fechar Caixa" action on the Caixa screen without an owner-only gate, and includes a confirmation dialog rather than a separate owner-approval step.

## Decision

Cashiers, in addition to owners, may final-close a cash session (`POST /cash-sessions/:id/close`). Closing produces the same immutable snapshot regardless of which role performed it (per ADR-003), with the closing user recorded via `closed_by_user_id`. To let a cashier make an informed close decision, `GET /cash-sessions/:id` returns the same operational totals and estimated profit to both roles for the session being closed. This does not change any other owner-only boundary: post-close adjustments (`POST /cash-sessions/:id/adjustments`) and the monthly/historical `/reports/*` endpoints remain owner-only.

## Alternatives Considered

### Alternative 1: Keep owner-only close (original ADR-004 decision)

- **Description**: Require owner confirmation for every close, as originally decided.
- **Pros**: Simplest permission boundary; keeps all financial confirmation with the owner.
- **Cons**: Blocks daily closing on days the owner isn't present, undermining the pilot's daily-closing success metric.
- **Why rejected**: Operational continuity for a single-owner pilot outweighs the marginal trust benefit.

### Alternative 2: Cashier prepares, owner confirms asynchronously

- **Description**: Cashier marks the session "ready to close"; the actual close and snapshot only happen when the owner later confirms, potentially from a different device.
- **Pros**: Keeps final financial confirmation with the owner without blocking cashier data entry.
- **Cons**: Introduces a new intermediate session state and notification flow not present in the reference UI; delays the close and report until the owner acts, which can span days if the owner is slow to confirm.
- **Why rejected**: Adds Phase 1 complexity beyond what the reference frontend and pilot timeline support.

## Consequences

### Positive

- Matches the reference UI's single-action close flow.
- Unblocks daily closing when the owner is away, supporting the pilot's daily-closing success metric.
- Keeps the immutable-snapshot and owner-only-adjustment model from ADR-003 intact; only the close action's permission changes.

### Negative

- Widens cashier visibility to include the current session's estimated profit (previously owner-only), though still scoped to the single session being closed, not historical reports.
- Removes the owner as a required check before a day's numbers are finalized, relying entirely on post-close adjustments to catch mistakes.

### Risks

- A cashier could close a session with incorrect totals since owner review is no longer required. Mitigation: keep the closing confirmation prompt, and rely on owner-only post-close adjustments (ADR-003) as the correction path.
- Cashiers seeing estimated profit could be considered sensitive in some pilots. Mitigation: keep this visibility scoped to the current open/just-closed session only; monthly and historical reports stay owner-only.

## Implementation Notes

Update role guards on `POST /cash-sessions/:id/close` to accept both `OWNER` and `CASHIER`. Update `GET /cash-sessions/:id` to return full operational totals and estimated profit for both roles. Leave `POST /cash-sessions/:id/adjustments` and all `/reports/*` endpoints owner-only. Record `closed_by_user_id` on the session for audit purposes.
